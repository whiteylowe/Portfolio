Case Study Overview

Spatial Companion explores an alternative to utility-driven AR. Instead of appearing on demand, the system exists as a persistent presence. Interaction is driven through motion, behavior, and attention, not interface density.

Problem
AR systems are optimized for quick access, not long-term presence. Entities appear and disappear, limiting continuity and emotional readability.
Approach
A stylized companion acts as the interface. Motion, state changes, and placement become the communication layer. Behavior replaces UI elements.
Outcome
A system where presence carries meaning. Stability, timing, and motion create a readable interaction model without overlays.

Perceptual Interface Principles

Clarity emerges from stability, timing, and behavioral hierarchy rather than visual density.

  • Motion relationships guide attention more effectively than persistent UI chrome.
  • Presence requires continuity across idle, active, and transitional states.
  • In lightweight AR, the environment is part of the interface grammar.
The companion is not a mascot layered onto space. It is a probe into how interface behavior can feel embodied, ambient, and emotionally legible within a shared physical environment.
