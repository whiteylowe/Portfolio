Overview

The apparatus does not simply record space. It reconstructs it.

Investigations into vision as a constructed process across mechanical, spatial, and biological systems.

Campus Obscura is an architectural research project examining how perception is not a passive reception of space but an active construction — shaped by the instruments, bodies, and systems through which space is seen.

"The camera obscura does not reveal the world as it is. It reveals the world as the apparatus allows it to be seen."

The project spans physical making, computational simulation, and phenomenological inquiry — tracing how spatial experience is mediated, distorted, and reconstructed across mechanical, photographic, and biological systems.

Research Method
01
Physical Making
Handbuilt optical instruments — pinhole cameras, water lenses, aperture mechanisms — as research objects. The apparatus itself becomes a site of inquiry.
02
Photographic Investigation
Long-exposure pinhole photography of the UF campus. Images that record not a moment but a duration — accumulations of spatial experience over time.
03
Computational Simulation
Real-time environments modelling light, shadow, and perceptual distortion. Translating phenomenological inquiry into spatial simulation.
04
Phenomenological Framing
Grounded in Merleau-Ponty and Eisenstein — the body as perceptual instrument, space as sequence, vision as active reconstruction rather than passive recording.
